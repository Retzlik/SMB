# 5. Go-To-Market Strategy

## 5.1 Channel Architecture

**Layer 1 — Outbound Sales (60% of Y1 acquisition):** Cold calling via professional registries, multi-channel cadence (phone, SMS, email, LinkedIn).

**Layer 2 — SEO Content (25% of Y1 acquisition):** Vertical-specific blog targeting low-KD keywords, driving organic traffic to free assessment.

**Layer 3 — Paid Retargeting + Problem-Aware Ads (15%):** Google Display retargeting (€0.10–0.40 CPC), Meta job-title targeting (€0.50–1.00), YouTube pre-roll (€0.01–0.05/view), problem-aware Search Ads (€0.50–2.50 CPC). Agency-intent keywords (€3–9 CPC) are unviable at this ARPU.

## 5.2 Outbound Regulatory Framework

**Germany:** B2B cold calling allowed under "presumed consent" (mutmaßliche Einwilligung, §7 UWG). Product must be relevant to the business — selling marketing to a business with a public GBP and few reviews meets this bar. Cold email effectively requires double opt-in — not viable as first touch. Strategy: phone-first.

**UK:** Most permissive. B2B cold calling and emailing both allowed under PECR with legitimate interest. Strategy: full multi-channel.

**Netherlands:** B2B cold calling allowed with opt-out. Cold email to generic addresses (info@) allowed. Strategy: phone-first + email to generic addresses.

**Nordics (SE, DK, NO, FI):** B2B cold calling generally permitted in all four countries. Sweden: scrub against NIX-Telefon. Strategy: phone-first. Swedish market via Innovative Sales AB.

**Belgium, Luxembourg:** B2B cold calling allowed with opt-out. No national B2B DNC list (Belgium). Strategy: phone-first.

## 5.3 Outbound Sales Benchmarks and Economics

Based on 2025–2026 data (Optifai N=939, Cognism, SalesHive):

- Cold call to meeting: average 2.5% (1 per 40 dials), top performers 5–8%
- UK market success rate: 8%. European market: 6%. EU markets 15–20% higher than US.
- SDR benchmark: 8–10 meetings/month (median), 12–15 (top). 50–80 calls/day.
- Best windows: 8–9am and 4–5pm local (+47% connect rate)
- Multichannel (3+ channels): 287% more responses vs. single channel

**Unit economics per SDR:** 60 dials/day, 12% connect rate = 7 conversations. 15% booking rate = ~1 meeting/day = 18–22/month. 25% close rate = 4–5 customers/month. At €4,500–5,500 fully loaded cost: direct CAC €900–1,375. Blended with inbound (€0 marginal cost): target €100–200.

# 7. Brand Strategy and Identity

## 7.1 The Seven Strategic Decisions

The brand strategy was developed through a structured process modelled on leading consultancy methodology (Wolff Olins, Ragged Edge, Pentagram). Seven sequential decisions, where each constrains the next.

### Decision 1: Category Design

**Karl creates a new category: the business agent.** Not a platform you use. Not an agency you hire. An agent that works for you. The prospect's question becomes: "Do I hire a person, hire an agency, buy 6 different tools, or get an agent?" When framed that way, "get an agent" wins on simplicity, cost, and effort every time.

### Decision 2: Brand Tension

**"Brilliant at your craft. Invisible online."** You became a dentist to help patients, not to manage Google reviews at midnight. You became an electrician to fix problems, not to figure out Instagram. The gap between what you deserve and what you're getting is the injustice Karl exists to fix.

### Decision 3: Challenger Archetype

**Substance disruptor. Tone champion.** The disruption is in the substance: transparent pricing, no lock-in, competitive data that reveals the gap. The tone is warm, competent, understated. Wise's playbook: the facts are loud so the voice can be calm. The cold call opens with a specific competitive insight (disruptor energy that creates urgency). The ongoing relationship is warm, supportive, and results-focused (empathetic energy that drives retention).

### Decision 4: Brand Promise

**More customers. Zero effort.** Grounded in what every successful SMB platform has proven works (Broadly, Thryv, Podium all lead with "more customers"). The difference from competitors: they give you tools to get customers. Karl gets you customers. The promise is functional and provable, not aspirational brand language.

### Decision 5: Brand Personality

**Competent. Direct. Warm. Proactive. European.**

- Competent, not clever — delivers, doesn't show off, no jargon
- Direct, not aggressive — tells you your competitor has more reviews, without sugarcoating or alarming
- Warm, not familiar — professional colleague you trust, not a buddy
- Proactive, not passive — finds problems, fixes them, tells you what she did
- European, not American — understated confidence, no "crushing it" or "growth hacking"

### Decision 6: Verbal Identity

**Tagline:** "You run the business. Karl grows it."

Two sentences, seven words: (1) respects the business owner's expertise, (2) defines Karl's role as growth, (3) implies zero effort from the owner.

**Vertical tagline framework:**

- Healthcare: "You treat patients. Karl fills your schedule."
- Professional services: "You advise clients. Karl finds new ones."
- Home & trade services: "You fix the pipes. Karl fills your calendar."
- Automotive & mobility: "You teach them to drive. Karl brings them to your door."

**German adaptation:** "Du führst dein Unternehmen. Karl lässt es wachsen."

**Voice rules:**

- First person always ("I" not "we" not "Karl's platform")
- Numbers before narratives ("You got 12 new reviews" before "which is great because...")
- Present and past tense ("I fixed" / "I'm running" — never future-aspirational)
- No jargon ("Your business shows up on Google" not "improved SERP visibility")
- No exclamation marks in reporting
- Specific over general ("Your competitor at Hauptstraße 14 has 89 reviews" not "competitors have more reviews")

### Decision 7: Visual Identity — 2C-iii Nordic Architect

**One colour. Total confidence.** Forest green is the only colour in the system. Everything else is ink, stone, and gray. The design disappears so the substance can speak.

**Colour palette:**

| Name | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Forest | #2A4636 | The only colour. Logo, primary CTAs, Karl's accent in headlines, agent status indicators |
| Ink | #1A1C1A | Primary text, headlines, dark UI elements |
| Mid | #787874 | Secondary text, captions, descriptions |
| Border | #C4C3BE | Borders, dividers, input fields |
| Warm gray | #E6E5E1 | Card backgrounds, section separation, pills, badges |
| Stone | #F4F4F1 | Primary surface background |
| Paper | #FAFAF8 | Lightest background, content areas |

No accent colour. No peach, no gold, no brass. Forest green appears only where Karl speaks. Vertical differentiation uses tinted stone backgrounds, not accent colours.

**Typography:**

| Role | Font | Weight | Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| Display / Headlines | Instrument Serif (Google Fonts) | 400 | 28–32px |
| Headline accent | Instrument Serif italic | 400 italic | Same as display — used for Karl's words |
| Body / UI | Libre Franklin (Google Fonts) | 400, 500 | 14–16px |
| Small / Captions | Libre Franklin | 400 | 12px |
| Micro / Labels | Libre Franklin | 500 | 10px (uppercase, letter-spaced 0.08em) |

Instrument Serif provides editorial authority — the quiet confidence of a financial newspaper. Libre Franklin provides Swiss precision in body text — crisper and more architectural than DM Sans.

**Logo:**

- Mark: Rounded square (border-radius: 8px) in forest green, containing Instrument Serif "L" in stone
- Wordmark: "Karl" in Instrument Serif 400, tracked at -0.02em
- On light: Forest mark + ink wordmark
- On dark: Stone mark (forest "L") + stone wordmark
- On forest background: Inverted stone mark + stone wordmark

**Components:**

- Buttons: 4px border-radius. Primary = forest background, stone text. Secondary = transparent, forest text, forest border. Tertiary = transparent, ink text, border-color border.
- Cards: 4px radius. Stone (#F4F4F1) background with 1px warm gray border.
- Pills/badges: 4px radius. Warm gray background, ink text. Forest background for active/selected states.
- Inputs: 4px radius. Paper background, border-color border, mid placeholder text.

**Design principles:**

1. **One colour means every use matters.** Forest green is rare and intentional — it marks where Karl speaks, acts, or appears. If everything is green, nothing is.
2. **Substance disruptor, tone champion.** The design is restrained. The message cuts. Let the competitive data, the pricing transparency, and the results speak.
3. **European architectural restraint.** Sharp 4px corners, not rounded 10px. Generous whitespace. One serif, one sans. No decorative elements. Think Scandinavian architecture studio, not American SaaS.
4. **The agent is the brand.** Monthly reports, status indicators, review counts, competitor comparisons — the product IS the visual identity. Data and results are design elements.
5. **Cross-market coherence.** The monochrome palette and restrained typography travel perfectly across English, German, Swedish, and Dutch without cultural friction.
